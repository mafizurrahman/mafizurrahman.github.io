About Me

I am a PhD student in Electrical Engineering at Prairie View A&M University, working under the guidance of Prof. Dr. Lijun Qian. My research focuses on Machine Learning, Analog Computing, Deep Learning, and Neurocomputing.

My current work involves converting pretrained deep neural networks to analog for image classification while maintaining high accuracy on benchmark datasets. I've collaborated with Sandia Labs to convert PyTorch-based DNNs for multi-frame super-resolution to analog DNNs.

Previously, I completed my M.Sc in Computer Science at Prairie View A&M University with a perfect 4.0 GPA, where my thesis focused on the "Comparative Analysis Of Inference Performance Of Pretrained Deep Learning Models In Analog Accelerators."
